# American Legion Post 579 — Military Branch History

## THE AMERICAN LEGION
**Founded:** March 15–17, 1919 — Paris, France  
**Chartered by Congress:** September 16, 1919  
**Mission:** A patriotic veterans service organization dedicated to mutual helpfulness, national security, strong communities, and Americanism.

The American Legion was born out of World War I. Veterans gathering in Paris proposed an organization that would advocate for servicemembers, care for the wounded, and honor the fallen. Since then, it has grown to over 2 million members across more than 12,000 posts worldwide — including **Post 579**, serving this community with pride.

---

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES ARMY
**Founded:** June 14, 1775 — Continental Congress, Philadelphia, PA  
**Colors:** Black & Gold  
**Motto:** "This We'll Defend"

The Army is the oldest and largest branch of the U.S. Armed Forces. Established before American independence, it was created to fight the British during the Revolutionary War under General George Washington. The Army has fought in every major American conflict — from the Battle of Bunker Hill to the sands of Iraq and Afghanistan. It is responsible for land-based military operations, occupying and holding territory, and protecting U.S. interests at home and abroad. Today, the Army numbers over 450,000 active duty soldiers supported by the National Guard and Army Reserve.

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES NAVY
**Founded:** October 13, 1775 — Continental Congress  
**Colors:** Navy Blue & Gold  
**Motto:** "Forged by the Sea"

The U.S. Navy was established just four months after the Army, created to harass British supply lines during the Revolution. From the wooden frigates of John Paul Jones to the nuclear-powered aircraft carriers of today, the Navy has always projected American power across the world's oceans. It controls sea lanes, launches air strikes from carriers, operates submarines, conducts amphibious assaults, and maintains strategic nuclear deterrence. The Navy is present in every ocean on Earth, every single day.

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES MARINE CORPS
**Founded:** November 10, 1775 — Tun Tavern, Philadelphia, PA  
**Colors:** Scarlet & Gold  
**Motto:** "Semper Fidelis" (Always Faithful)

The Marine Corps is America's rapid-response force. Born in a tavern two days before the Navy, Marines were originally sharpshooters stationed in the rigging of Navy ships. Today they serve as America's "911 Force" — the first boots on the ground in a crisis. Marines stormed the beaches of Iwo Jima, fought through the frozen Chosin Reservoir in Korea, and led some of the most intense combat in Fallujah, Iraq. Their ethos of toughness, discipline, and unit cohesion is unmatched. "Once a Marine, always a Marine."

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
**Founded:** September 18, 1947 — National Security Act  
**Colors:** Ultramarine Blue & Maize Gold  
**Motto:** "Aim High... Fly-Fight-Win"

The Air Force traces its roots to the Army Signal Corps balloon units of the Civil War and the Wright Brothers' first flight in 1903. After World War II proved that air power was decisive in modern warfare, Congress established the Air Force as a separate branch in 1947. The Air Force controls the skies, conducts strategic bombing, provides close air support for ground troops, operates satellites and space assets, and maintains the nuclear triad alongside the Navy. From the P-51 Mustang to the F-22 Raptor to the B-2 Spirit stealth bomber — the Air Force dominates the air.

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES COAST GUARD
**Founded:** August 4, 1790 — Revenue Marine, signed by Alexander Hamilton  
**Colors:** Racing Stripe Red, White & Blue  
**Motto:** "Semper Paratus" (Always Ready)

The Coast Guard is the oldest continuous seagoing service in U.S. history, established by Treasury Secretary Alexander Hamilton to enforce customs and tax laws at sea. Today it serves a dual mission — in peacetime it operates under the Department of Homeland Security, protecting U.S. coastlines, conducting search and rescue, enforcing maritime law, and combating drug trafficking. In wartime, it transfers to Department of Defense control and has served in every American conflict including both World Wars, Vietnam, and the Gulf War. The Coast Guard saves thousands of lives every year.

---

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES SPACE FORCE
**Founded:** December 20, 2019 — National Defense Authorization Act  
**Colors:** Delta Blue & Spacecraft White  
**Motto:** "Semper Supra" (Always Above)

The Space Force is the newest branch of the U.S. military and the first new branch since 1947. It was established to protect American assets in space — satellites, GPS systems, missile warning systems, and communications networks that the entire military depends on. Space has become a domain of warfare just like land, sea, and air. The Space Force organizes, trains, and equips Guardians to ensure the U.S. maintains superiority above the atmosphere.
