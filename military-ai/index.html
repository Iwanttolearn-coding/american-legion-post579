The Six Branches of Service

Every member of Post 579 served. We honor all branches equally — their history, their sacrifices, and the veterans who carried those colors home to San Antonio.

U.S. Army

United States Army

Est. June 14, 1775 — Our Nation's Oldest Branch

The U.S. Army traces its founding to the Second Continental Congress, formed 14 years before the Constitution. From Valley Forge to Normandy, from Korea to Fallujah — the Army has answered every call.

Post 579 is home to Army veterans who served in infantry, armor, artillery, aviation, intelligence, logistics, and special operations across every major conflict of the 20th and 21st centuries.

  • Branch ColorBlack & Gold
  • MottoThis We'll Defend
  • Key ConflictsWWII, Korea, Vietnam, Gulf War, Iraq, Afghanistan
  • Fort Near SAFort Sam Houston / JBSA
U.S. Marine Corps

United States Marine Corps

Est. November 10, 1775 — The Few. The Proud.

Born at Tun Tavern in Philadelphia, the Marines were established four months after the Army. Known for their fierce warrior culture, the Corps leads amphibious assaults and rapid response missions worldwide.

Marine veterans at Post 579 carry the traditions of Belleau Wood, Iwo Jima, Chosin Reservoir, Hue City, and Fallujah — battles where the motto "Semper Fidelis" was written in blood.

  • Branch ColorScarlet & Gold
  • MottoSemper Fidelis (Always Faithful)
  • Key BattlesIwo Jima, Chosin, Hue City, Fallujah
  • Unique RoleAmphibious assault, rapid response

U.S. Navy

United States Navy

Est. October 13, 1775 — Command of the Seas

The Continental Navy was established two months before the Marine Corps to defend American waters against the British Royal Navy. Today, the U.S. Navy operates the most powerful fleet in human history.

Post 579 honors Navy veterans who served on aircraft carriers, destroyers, submarines, amphibious ships, and at naval air stations — including many who served at Naval Air Station Chase Field in nearby Beeville, TX.

  • Branch ColorNavy Blue & Gold
  • MottoForged by the Sea
  • Key ConflictsBattle of Midway, Pacific Theatre, Persian Gulf
  • TX ConnectionNAS Corpus Christi, NAS JRB Fort Worth
U.S. Air Force

United States Air Force

Est. September 18, 1947 — Air, Space & Cyberspace

Originally part of the Army Air Forces, the Air Force became an independent branch in 1947 under the National Security Act. From the Berlin Airlift to stealth bombers over Baghdad, the Air Force has dominated the skies for over 75 years.

Lackland Air Force Base — the only entry-level training installation for USAF — sits right here in San Antonio, making Post 579 home to hundreds of Air Force veterans who began their service in our city.

  • Branch ColorUltramarine Blue & Gold
  • MottoAim High… Fly-Fight-Win
  • SA ConnectionLackland AFB / JBSA — Basic Training
  • Key RoleAir superiority, global strike, ISR

U.S. Coast Guard

United States Coast Guard

Est. August 4, 1790 — Always Ready (Semper Paratus)

The oldest continuous seagoing service in the United States, the Coast Guard was established by Alexander Hamilton as the Revenue Cutter Service. It has participated in every major U.S. conflict, including D-Day, where Coast Guard-crewed vessels landed Army troops on Normandy beaches.

Coast Guard veterans at Post 579 served in search and rescue, maritime law enforcement, port security, and national defense — often in conditions no other branch faces.

  • Branch ColorRed, White & Blue
  • MottoSemper Paratus (Always Ready)
  • Key RoleSAR, port security, maritime law
  • Fun FactOldest U.S. seagoing service
U.S. Space Force

United States Space Force

Est. December 20, 2019 — Guardians of the High Frontier

The newest branch of the U.S. Armed Forces and the first new branch in over 70 years, the Space Force was established to protect American interests in space — GPS satellites, missile warning systems, space domain awareness, and beyond.

As the Space Force continues to grow, Post 579 stands ready to welcome Space Force veterans — the Guardians — into our family. Several of our members transitioned from Air Force space commands that became foundational to this new branch.

  • Branch ColorDeep Space Navy & Silver
  • MottoSemper Supra (Always Above)
  • Key RoleGPS, missile warning, satellite ops
  • First BasePeterson SFB, Colorado
