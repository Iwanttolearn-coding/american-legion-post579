About Post 579

Mrs. Carden Post — History, Mission, Officers, and Values

History of Post 579

American Legion Post 579 — the Mrs. Carden Post — is a chartered post of The American Legion, the world's largest veterans service organization. We proudly serve the San Antonio, Texas community and the brave men and women who have worn the uniform of the United States Armed Forces.

Post 579 carries its name in honor of Mrs. Carden, a distinguished figure whose commitment to veterans and community service inspired the founding members of this post. Her legacy lives on in every service project, every benefit claim filed, and every veteran we assist.

The American Legion itself was founded in Paris, France in March 1919 by World War I veterans, and chartered by the United States Congress on September 16, 1919. It has fought for veterans' rights ever since — including drafting and advocating for the original GI Bill (1944), which transformed American society and created the modern middle class.

Today, Post 579 continues that mission in San Antonio — advocating for veterans, supporting military families, serving the youth of our community, and promoting the values of Americanism that define this great nation.

Our Mission & Four Pillars
⚑

Veterans Affairs

Advocating for veteran healthcare, disability benefits, housing, employment, and the full spectrum of VA services every veteran has earned.

🛡

National Security

Supporting a strong national defense, honoring those currently serving, and advocating for proper funding of the U.S. Armed Forces.

★

Americanism

Promoting patriotism, civic education, and the values of the U.S. Constitution that veterans fought to protect and defend.

♥

Youth & Communities

Investing in the next generation through youth programs, scholarships, community service, and building stronger neighborhoods.
