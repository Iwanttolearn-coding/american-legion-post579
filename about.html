About Post 579

Committed to Veterans
Since 1976

American Legion Bicentennial Post 579 — Mrs. Carden Post — Lackland Terrace, San Antonio, Texas.

Our Mission

For God and Country

American Legion Post 579 exists to serve those who served. We provide fellowship, veteran support, community service, patriotic programs, and opportunities for members to continue serving with purpose and honor.

Our charter mandates four pillars of service: Veterans, Defense, Americanism, and Youth & Children. Every program and activity at Post 579 flows from these core commitments.

🎖️

Veterans

Advocating for VA benefits, healthcare, and dignity for all who served.

🛡️

Defense

Supporting a strong national defense and honoring active duty service.

🦅

Americanism

Promoting patriotism, civic education, and love of country.

🌟

Youth

Investing in the next generation through Boys State, Girls State, and scholarships.

Our Story

History of Post 579

1976

Post Chartered

American Legion Bicentennial Post 579 is chartered in the Lackland Terrace neighborhood of San Antonio during the nation's Bicentennial year. The post is named in honor of Mrs. Carden for her dedication to the veterans community.

1980s

Growth & Programs

Post 579 expands its membership and launches community programs including Boys State sponsorships, patriotic ceremonies, and veteran assistance services.

1990s

Gulf War Era Service

Post 579 welcomes a new generation of Gulf War veterans and expands its VA benefits counseling services to meet growing demand.

2001–Present

Post-9/11 Service

Post 579 serves veterans of OEF, OIF, and subsequent conflicts, growing its support network for combat veterans, their families, and the wider community.

2026

Serving Today

Post 579 continues to serve San Antonio veterans and families with honor, launching new digital tools, community events, and expanded outreach programs.
